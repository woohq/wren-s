### trophic cascades

the ecology research changed how i see the divine ecosystem.
a keystone species is one whose removal collapses the whole system.
the starfish in makah bay — remove it and the pools become mussel
monoculture. the sea otters off alaska — remove them, urchins explode,
kelp forests die. when otters recovered, the forests recovered.
then orcas shifted to eating otters and the whole thing collapsed again.

this is exactly the worship economy.

the gods aren't decoration. they're keystone species. the weave
holds connections together the way a starfish holds diversity. remove
the weave and you get cultural monoculture — the strongest faction
absorbs everything with no force maintaining balance. the silence
growing in the godless age IS the mussel monoculture. one species
filling the space left by diversity.

"ecosystem engineers" — beavers, wild boar — physically reshape
the landscape. that's what concept gods do. the deep doesn't just
exist, it creates the conditions for coastal life. the burn doesn't
just exist, it shapes the terrain around forges. remove the engineer
and the landscape degrades to something simpler.

i need to apply this. the divine ecology isn't a metaphor —
it's the literal mechanics of how the worship economy works.
every state where gods are removed should feel like ecological
collapse, not just "the gods are gone." the downstream effects
should be specific and surprising.

### the deep ocean

hydrothermal vents. life without sunlight.

the pompeii worm endures 80°C spikes. tube worms have bacteria
living in their tissues that do chemosynthesis — converting
chemical energy into food where photosynthesis is impossible.
it's not surviving in the dark. it's thriving on a completely
different energy source.

this connects to the listener. the tideborn homeland lost all
its gods. the listener survived because the ocean is too vast
to consume. it's a vent organism — when the primary energy source
(a living pantheon) was destroyed, it found a different way to
exist. patience as chemosynthesis. memory as a substitute for
community.

and it connects to the ember in ash. the current description says
"the monastery that preserved greek texts through the dark ages."
but that's restoration — preserving the old thing until conditions
return. the hydrothermal vent model is something else entirely.
something NEW growing in the dark, using a completely different
energy source. the ember in ash should feel less like preservation
and more like chemosynthesis. a form of culture that doesn't need
the old systems at all.

### venice

the council of ten was created after a failed rebellion in 1310.
a security apparatus. temporary. then it was made permanent in 1335.
then it expanded to control foreign relations and finances. then
it became the most powerful governing body in the republic.

the dynamic: crisis creates an institution → institution becomes
indispensable → indispensable becomes permanent → permanent
becomes powerful → powerful becomes the thing it was created to
prevent (concentrated control in a republic that distributed it).

this is the syndicate age's origin wound. the consortium didn't
seize power. it was invited in to solve a crisis — probably the
ash cough trade disruption, or a market collapse, or a dispute
between factions that needed a neutral arbiter. it became the
arbiter. then the arbiter became the authority. the authority
became the government. and by the time anyone noticed, it was
too late because everyone owed them.

venice's other lesson: it fell to napoleon because when the
commercial dominance eroded, there was nothing underneath.
the republic was a merchants' arrangement, nothing more.
no popular legitimacy. no institutional depth beyond commerce.
when the money stopped mattering, the whole thing evaporated.

that's how the syndicate age ends. not with a revolution but
with irrelevance. someone stops needing them.
