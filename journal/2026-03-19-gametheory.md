## the insight

cooperation emerges from selfishness when:
1. interactions are REPEATED (not one-shot)
2. players have MEMORY (they remember what you did)
3. the future matters more than the present (the shadow of the future)
4. the strategy is NICE (don't defect first), RETALIATORY
   (punish defection), FORGIVING (return to cooperation
   after punishment), and CLEAR (be predictable)

tit for tat: cooperate first, then mirror.
four lines of code. simplest strategy submitted.
won the tournament against complex exploitative strategies
because it was nice, retaliatory, forgiving, and clear.

## mapping to vestige

**the player-god is playing iterated prisoner's dilemma
across centuries.**

each vessel is a "round." each faction remembers what the
player's previous vessels did. trust is built across lifetimes.
the player who cooperates consistently builds the deepest
alliances. the player who exploits burns bridges permanently.

**the factions are playing with each other too:**
- golden compromise = mutual cooperation. tit for tat working.
- the betrayal = defection after cooperation. torren broke the
  pattern. the question: was it defection or self-defense?
- merchant wars = mutual defection spiral. dex and brennan
  mirroring each other's violence.
- regulated peace = enforced cooperation. not tit-for-tat but
  structural coercion. it prevents defection but also prevents
  genuine trust.
- the long peace = cooperation sustained so long that nobody
  remembers it's cooperation (they think it's just how things are).

**the noise problem is the most interesting:**

"what happens when someone cooperates but the other side
THINKS they defected?"

that's the betrayal. torren sealed the forges. from HIS
perspective: defense (cooperation with his own values).
from renn's perspective: defection (breaking the compromise).
same action. different reading. cooperation broke not because
someone was selfish but because the signal was MISREAD.

in game theory, noise is the killer of cooperation. in a
noisy environment, tit-for-tat degrades into alternating
retaliation — you think they defected so you defect, they
think YOU defected so they defect, and the spiral never
recovers.

the solution in game theory: be more FORGIVING. generous
tit-for-tat occasionally cooperates even after defection.
this breaks the retaliation spiral at the cost of being
exploitable.

in vestige: the player-god is the noise filter. the god
can see both sides (information asymmetry principle from
moral-complexity.md). the god knows torren's defense AND
renn's perception. the god's vessel can be the FORGIVING
move — the one who cooperates after a misread signal,
breaking the spiral.

that's the player's deepest role: not choosing sides but
FILTERING NOISE. making the signal clear so cooperation
can resume.
