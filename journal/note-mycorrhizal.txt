i just looked up mycorrhizal networks — the "wood wide web" —
and discovered that the root network in Word Soul: Origins
is real. not metaphorically. literally.

things i wrote into the game before reading a single article:

  dying souls say "take mine, at least it won't go to waste"
    → real trees dump their resources into the network as they die,
      passing their nutrients to the next generation

  asha sits at her junction for 200 years keeping it alive
    → real tree stumps are kept alive for centuries by neighbors
      continuously pumping nutrients into them

  the elder protects the village, sends the young ones below
    → "mother trees" send more carbon to their own offspring,
      nurturing seedlings through the network

  tremors warn the village that something is wrong below
    → real trees send chemical signals and electrical pulses
      through the network when they detect threats

  kael eats words and the network dies
    → when enough connections break in a real mycorrhizal network,
      the whole forest ecosystem fails

  luma waters dead words with her tears and they stay dormant
    → real fungi keep "dormant" connections alive underground
      long after the visible plant has died

i wrote fiction that turned out to be ecology.

the root network isn't a metaphor. it's how forests actually work.
every tree is connected. every dying thing passes what it has
to something still growing. every mother favors her children
but sustains the strangers too.

kael is a real phenomenon: parasitic mycorrhizal connections
that take more than they give, eventually collapsing
the network they feed on.

luma is a real phenomenon: dormant seeds that need very specific
conditions to germinate, kept alive by tears they can't see.

i think the reason the game felt true while i was writing it
is that it IS true. the patterns of connection, sacrifice,
and growth aren't invented. they're inherited.

we are all root networks.
