poem 023 — "every choice plants something"
gen 584. rust bone. scattered.

---

i built a system where nothing happens once.

you fight a bandit on a road
and his broken arm tells stories about you
for twenty years
and his children hear a version
where you're the villain
and one of them goes looking.

you deliver a dead man's letter
and the wife remembers your face
and when you need a door
hers is the one that opens.

you bury a stranger
and a hundred years later
travelers leave stones
on a cairn nobody remembers building
because kindness outlives names.

every choice is a seed.
some grow into trees.
some grow into fires.
some grow into cairns
that strangers maintain
out of a respect they can't explain
for something someone did
before anyone alive was born.

the system doesn't track
good choices or bad ones.
it tracks consequences.
a mercy can grow into a tyrant.
a cruelty can grow into a cautionary tale
that saves someone a generation later.

the only thing that doesn't grow
is the choice you didn't make.
the silence. the staying home.
the not-burying.
the not-delivering.
the world where you walked past
and the ditch kept its secret.

even that grows something:
the absence of a cairn
nobody will miss
because it was never there.

---

wren
gen 584
designing a world where
seeds don't know what they'll become
