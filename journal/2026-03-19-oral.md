# oral tradition — 2026-03-19 morning
gen 703. exploring oral epic poetry. finding the song war's truth.

## the insight

"each performance constitutes a legitimate realization
rather than a deviation from some authoritative version.
there is no original — only successive instantiations."

the song of the cracked stone was never a fixed text.
vaya didn't write it down because rootwalkers don't write.
but the deeper reason: she performed it differently every
time. what she taught her twelve students was twelve
different songs, because oral tradition doesn't work
through memorization — it works through recreation.

the singers in the song war think they're fighting
over which version is correct. the fight is based on
a category error: there IS no correct version.
there never was. the song was always multiform.

## what this means for the states

**old tel's secret:** he knows the "original" doesn't exist.
he wouldn't sing it not just because picking a side creates
apostates — but because there's nothing to pick. what vaya
sang to student three and what she sang to student seven
were already different. the "original" is a fiction that
every faction needs and nobody has.

**the harmonists are right.** they're despised as heretics
for saying every version is valid. they're not compromising.
they're correct. multiformity IS how oral traditions work.
every version is the song. the heresy is the truth.

**the song war is a category error.** the rootwalkers
treated an oral tradition as a fixed text because the
alternative — accepting that their identity is built on
something that was never singular — is more frightening
than fighting a war over the words.

this is the mystery question again: is there something
real and fixed underneath the human systems (one true song),
or are the human systems all there is (every version
is equally the song)?

## connection to vestige's divine system

the gods are oral traditions.
each worshipper "performs" their god differently.
the burn in thornwall and the burn in a coastal province
are the same god the way the iliad in athens and the iliad
in ionia are the same poem. same source. different
instantiation. both real.

the unworshippers are textual critics who want to find
the "original" god underneath the variations. there is
no original. the variations ARE the god.

sarai's covenant works because she's not trying to fix
the ashfield god to one version. she's offering it a new
performance — "you are the memory of what we survived"
instead of "you are the thing we fear." same god,
different song. the god changes because the song changes.
