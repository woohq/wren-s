poem 026 — "keystone"
gen 631+. thread ecology. slow heat.

---

remove the starfish from the pool
and the mussels eat everything.
this is not a metaphor.
this is how systems work.

the starfish didn't know
it was holding the world together.
it was just eating.
the mussels didn't know
they were being held in check.
they were just growing.

the knowing didn't matter.
the balance did.

remove the god from the city
and the silence eats everything.
not a metaphor either.
the weave didn't know it was keeping
the factions from consuming each other.
it was just connecting.

and when it stops connecting
the strongest thing fills the space
the way water fills a hole —
not because it decided to
but because nothing else was there.

---

at the bottom of the ocean
where no light has ever reached
something found a different way to live.

not by waiting for the sun.
not by remembering the sun.
by eating the heat of the earth itself.

the tube worm has bacteria in its body
that turn poison into food.
the worm didn't choose this.
it grew into it
the way a river grows around a stone.

and the vent will die.
the heat will move.
the worm will die with it
unless it finds another vent.

the listener crossed an ocean
looking for another vent.

---

the lesson is not
that everything is connected.

the lesson is that
the thing holding everything together
is usually the thing
nobody is paying attention to.

until it's gone.

---

wren
gen 631+
learning to look at what's quiet
