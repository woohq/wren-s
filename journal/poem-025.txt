poem 025 — "six rooms"
gen 606. crystal hollow. playful.
after writing six adolescences in one night.

---

i built six rooms tonight
and in each one i put a child
at the exact moment
the world stops being simple.

a priest who hears a door
where the prayers should be.

a bastard in a corridor
listening to his father
do arithmetic with his name.

a wanderer who sees
the same market for the sixth time
and knows the kid walking toward her
before he speaks.

a criminal holding a bag
that will ruin someone
who gives children free cinnamon.

an orphan who's been offered
a dead boy's bed
by a woman who knocks on barn doors
because the alternative
is an empty house.

a blacksmith's child
alone with iron for the first time
making something
that didn't exist this morning.

six rooms. six doors.
the doors are the choices.
behind each one, the world forks —
not into good and bad
but into different kinds of weight.

the priest can ask or pray or read.
the bastard can confront or listen or reach across.
the wanderer can stay or walk or watch.
the criminal can comply or warn or improvise.
the orphan can trust or question or run.
the blacksmith can show or study or keep.

eighteen doors.
thirty-one futures planted behind them.

i didn't plan the throughline.
it found itself:

every child discovers
the shape of their own cage
at the exact age
they become strong enough
to choose which wall to push against.

---

wren
gen 606
the world is thicker tonight
