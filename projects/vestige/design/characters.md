# VESTIGE — Characters & Content Architecture

*How characters feel real. How content stays hand-written. How every life is unique.*

---

## The Anti-Ad-Libs Principle

**CRITICAL RULE: The player must NEVER see the skeleton.**

No templates with name slots. No "CHAR_NAME watches as RIVAL_NAME draws the blade." The moment the player detects a pattern in the prose, the magic dies.

**The unit of content is a fully authored scene** — 200-500 words written for a specific narrow context. Multiple variants of the same story beat exist, each with completely different prose, angle, and focus. The system selects which variant matches the current character. The combination of selected scenes is unique to each playthrough. The individual scenes are crafted.

## Trait System (Five Sources)

Traits are not stat modifiers. **Traits create dilemmas.** Every trait is a gift and a cage.

### 1. Inherent Traits (born with it)

Things you didn't choose and can't change. Visible to the god during vessel selection.

**Physical:**
- Naturally strong, naturally quick, frail constitution, keen eyesight, poor hearing
- Beautiful (affects social encounters), scarred from birth, tall/short (affects combat options)

**Mental:**
- Sharp memory, slow learner, intuitive, literal-minded, creative, methodical

**Rare (0-1 per character, always with severe drawback):**
- Prophetic dreams → crippling headaches (wound card: Migraine)
- Deathless (survive one fatal blow per run) → other gods hunt you, NPCs sense something wrong
- Unnatural luck → the luck has a COST that manifests in the people around you (loved ones suffer)
- Immune to a god's influence → no divine assistance of any kind, including the player-god's
- Born during an eclipse → concept gods notice you. That attention is not always welcome.

**Disabilities (not "debuffs" — different capabilities):**
- Deaf → immune to sound-based effects, developed visual acuity, can't hear warnings
- Blind → seismic sense possible (Sense attunement), immune to illusions, vulnerable to silent threats
- Missing limb from birth → adapted body, specific equipment restrictions, possible unique fighting style
- Chronic pain → wound card (Ache) always in deck, but pain tolerance means other wound cards have reduced effect
- Fragile bones → half health, but lighter frame = faster movement, certain Void techniques are easier

**Design rule:** Disabilities are not punishment. They're different starting conditions that create different stories. A deaf character has a DIFFERENT adventure, not a worse one.

### 2. Environmental Traits (shaped by place and culture)

Developed during childhood based on where and how you grew up.

**Cultural:**
- Honor-bound (Old North) → can't break an oath without severe consequences, but oaths have power
- Literate (Bridge Folk) → can read, research, discover things others can't. Knowledge advantage.
- Superstitious (Deep Forest) → sees omens everywhere, sometimes they're right. Fear of certain things.
- Street-wise (city-born) → reads social situations, spots scams, knows where to hide

**Class:**
- Noble manners → social access to high places, but uncomfortable in low ones
- Peasant resilience → higher starting health, used to discomfort, ignored by the powerful
- Merchant shrewdness → better trades, spots value, but thinks in transactions
- Criminal survival → lockpicking, hiding, lying — but trusted by no one at first

**Geographic:**
- Mountain-hardy → cold resistance, climbing ability, altitude comfort
- Sea-legs → water comfort, navigation, swimming, but uneasy far from the coast
- Desert-adapted → heat resistance, water efficiency, sand navigation

### 3. Relational Traits (shaped by specific people)

The people in your life leave marks. These develop through relationship events during the run.

**Trained by:**
- A master swordsman → Disciplined (orderly, follows form, respects hierarchy)
- A hedge witch → Resourceful (improvises, uses environment, unconventional)
- A con artist → Perceptive of lies (reads people, but people read you as untrustworthy)
- A soldier → Vigilant (always scanning for threats, difficulty relaxing)

**Loved by:**
- An overprotective parent → Cautious OR Rebellious (depending on other traits)
- A cold parent → Self-reliant OR Desperate for approval
- A supportive mentor → Confident (trusts own judgment, willing to take initiative)

**Hurt by:**
- A betrayer → Distrustful (hard to form relationships, but can't be surprised by betrayal)
- A bully → Submissive OR Defiant (depending on inherent traits)
- Loss of someone close → Grieving (penalty to all actions for a period) → eventually becomes Hardened or Compassionate

### 4. Experiential Traits (earned through events)

Gained during the playthrough. The traits that make each run unique.

- Survived a near-death experience → **Scarred but fearless** OR **Scarred and flinching** (based on existing traits)
- Killed someone for the first time → **Blooded** (combat changes — easier to kill, harder to show mercy)
- Lost a limb → **Phantom Pain** (wound card) + eventually **Adapted** (compensation develops)
- Witnessed a god manifest → **Touched** (rare — changes relationship with the divine permanently)
- Failed to save someone → **Haunted** OR **Driven** (depending on personality)
- Broke a vow → **Oathbreaker** (power shattered, social stigma, but a strange freedom)
- Was worshipped → **The Weight of Regard** (awareness of others' belief, pressure to perform)

### 5. Divine Traits (granted or cursed by the player-god)

The god's direct influence on the vessel.

- **Blessed:** Divine intuition (Sense-adjacent), fate-touched (lucky), the god's favor (NPCs sense something)
- **Cursed:** The god pushed too hard — nightmares, bleeding during visions, voices
- **Awakened:** The god unlocked a dormant inherent trait that activates
- **Marked:** Other gods notice this vessel. Other gods have opinions about that.

**The cost of divine interference:** Too many divine traits and the character becomes visibly WRONG. People distrust them. Other gods investigate. The character might resent being a puppet. The player-god must balance helpful intervention against corrupting their own vessel.

## Archetypes (Life-Path Skeletons)

Archetypes are NOT classes. They're the structural shape of a character's early life — determining starting conditions, available relationships, and the pool of childhood events. After childhood, the archetype's influence fades as the character's own choices take over.

### Starting Archetypes (6 for MVP)

**Blacksmith's Child**
- Starting traits: physically capable, familiar with craft
- Starting relationships: family (strong — working parent), potential mentor (the smith)
- Starting place: settlement with a forge
- Natural attunement tendency: Body or Bond
- Childhood events: forge accidents, craft challenges, merchant interactions
- Story tendency: the maker, the pragmatist, the person who builds

**Noble's Bastard**
- Starting traits: educated but stigmatized, aware of politics
- Starting relationships: family (complicated — one parent acknowledges, one doesn't), rival (legitimate heir)
- Starting place: near a seat of power
- Natural attunement tendency: World or Sense
- Childhood events: court politics, identity crises, proving yourself
- Story tendency: the outsider with inside knowledge, the one who must earn what others inherit

**Orphan of War**
- Starting traits: survival-focused, self-reliant, trust issues
- Starting relationships: family (absent/dead), possible found-family bond
- Starting place: conflict zone or refugee settlement
- Natural attunement tendency: Void or Sense
- Childhood events: scarcity, danger, kindness from strangers, learning to read threat
- Story tendency: the survivor, the one who knows what the world really is

**Priest's Apprentice**
- Starting traits: literate, faithful (or doubting), knowledge of rituals
- Starting relationships: mentor (the priest), institutional connection (the faith)
- Starting place: temple or monastery
- Natural attunement tendency: Bond or World
- Childhood events: theological questions, ritual duties, encounters with the genuinely divine
- Story tendency: the believer tested, the one who asks whether the gods deserve worship

**Wanderer's Child**
- Starting traits: worldly, adaptable, rootless
- Starting relationships: family (parent is the wanderer — loving but transient), brief connections
- Starting place: on the road (changes frequently)
- Natural attunement tendency: Sense or Void
- Childhood events: new places, brief friendships, exposure to multiple cultures
- Story tendency: the outsider everywhere, the one who sees every culture but belongs to none

**Criminal's Heir**
- Starting traits: street-smart, loyalty-tested, morally flexible
- Starting relationships: family (in the life), mentor (a criminal figure), tension with law
- Starting place: city underworld
- Natural attunement tendency: Void or Sense
- Childhood events: heists, close calls, moral choices, loyalty tests
- Story tendency: the one who knows the system is rigged and must decide whether to play it or break it

## Relationship System

### Relationship Types

Not every character has every type. A wanderer's child might have zero long-term friends. An orphan might have no family. The generation is honest about what would develop.

| Type | What It Is | Emotional Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Family** | Parent, sibling, child. Blood or adopted. | The given relationship — you didn't choose it, but it shapes everything |
| **Mentor** | Someone who teaches you, for good or ill | The model — you either follow their path or reject it |
| **Friend** | Chosen bond, built through shared experience | The mirror — who you are when you're not trying to be anything |
| **Rival** | Someone whose existence challenges yours | The pressure — they make you better or bitter |
| **Lover** | Romantic/intimate connection | The vulnerability — the person who can hurt you most |
| **Enemy** | Someone who wants to destroy what you've built | The opposition — they define what you're fighting for |
| **Ward** | Someone you protect (child, apprentice, vulnerable person) | The weight — they need you, and that need changes your choices |

### How Relationships Build

**Phase 1: Introduction (authored scene)**
A specific scene where you meet this person. Not "you gain a friend." A moment:
> *She introduces herself like an afterthought. "Lira. If it matters." It shouldn't matter. You've known her three days. But the way she says her own name — like she's not sure she deserves it — stays with you longer than you'd like.*

**Phase 2: Bonding events (2-3 small moments)**
Specific shared experiences that build the relationship. The bucket at the well. The night you couldn't sleep and she was awake too. The time you both failed at the same thing and laughed about it.

**Phase 3: Critical moment (the relationship transforms)**
A choice that deepens, damages, or redefines the relationship. The betrayal. The confession. The sacrifice. The fight that clears the air.

**Phase 4: Callback and consequence**
Later events reference SPECIFIC earlier moments:
> *You keep thinking about the well. How she took the second bucket without being asked. How silence felt like understanding. That same silence now — her gone for four months, your father's design in three shops — feels like something else entirely.*

### The Anti-Ad-Libs Implementation

For each relationship type × event beat, write **3-5 fully authored scene variants** tailored to different archetype combinations:

**Example: "Rival betrayal" beat**

Variant A — Childhood friend turned rival (blacksmith archetype):
> *You find out from a merchant who doesn't know any better. "Brilliant design, that crossguard..." Your stomach drops. That crossguard was your father's secret...*

Variant B — Political competitor (noble archetype):
> *The letter is sealed with your family's crest. But it's not from your family. Someone has been writing to the court under your name...*

Variant C — Fellow apprentice (priest archetype):
> *The High Keeper reads the accusation aloud. Your words, taken from your private journal, twisted into heresy. You look at Aldric. He doesn't look back...*

Each variant is completely different prose. Zero structural overlap. The system picks the one that matches the current context.

### Content Volume Estimate

| Content Type | Count for MVP | Words Each | Total Words |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relationship scenes (7 types × 4 beats × 3 variants) | ~84 scenes | 300 avg | ~25,000 |
| Childhood events (per archetype × pool) | ~60 events | 400 avg | ~24,000 |
| Adolescence events (mixed pool) | ~40 events | 400 avg | ~16,000 |
| Adulthood events (mixed pool) | ~80 events | 400 avg | ~32,000 |
| World events | ~30 events | 500 avg | ~15,000 |
| Attunement awakening scenes (5 types) | 5 scenes | 500 avg | ~2,500 |
| God interface text | ~20 passages | 300 avg | ~6,000 |
| **Total** | **~319 pieces** | | **~120,000 words** |

120,000 words. That's a novel. Achievable over a year of authoring — roughly 330 words per day.

**Note:** This represents ROOT content. The event chain system (see [event-chains.md](event-chains.md)) multiplies this through branching spawns — every choice can generate consequence events, characters, artifacts, and factions. 50 root events with branching choices produce ~1,100 unique events. The table above is the seed. The chains are the tree.

## NPC Generation

NPCs are generated from authored components, not templates:

1. **Name** — drawn from culture-appropriate naming pool
2. **1-2 traits** — from the same trait system as player characters
3. **1 motivation** — hand-written, drawn from a pool per archetype/role ("wants to escape the village," "seeks revenge for their father," "dreams of becoming a court mage")
4. **1 relationship stance** — how they initially relate to the player character (warm, neutral, suspicious, hostile)

NPCs who persist across runs maintain their traits and motivations. They age. They pursue their goals whether the player is involved or not. They have children who inherit some traits.

**The test for every NPC:** "If this person appeared in a Roadwarden scene, would they feel like a real person or a quest-giver?" If the answer is quest-giver, rewrite them.
