# VESTIGE — Power System

*Two systems, one world. Mortal power comes from the world. Divine power comes from belief. They are not the same force.*

---

## System 1: Mortal Power

### The Source

There is a fundamental energy woven into the fabric of the world. It flows through earth, air, water, living things — everything that exists has it. Every culture has a name for it:

- The Northern Clans call it **Earth-Blood**
- The Southern Empire calls it **Aether**
- The Ash Coast scholars call it **The Fundament**
- The Deep Forest peoples call it **The Hum**
- Common people call it **the Gift**, or have no name at all

The player discovers across runs that these are all the same thing. That realization — that the barbarian's "Earth-Blood" and the scholar's "Fundament" are identical — is itself a knowledge-unlock moment.

### The Foundation (shared by all practitioners)

Before any specialization, every practitioner must learn five basic disciplines. These are the alphabet. What you spell with them is your specialization.

| Discipline | What It Does | In Practice |
|---|---|---|
| **Sense** | Feel the energy in yourself and the world around you. The first step. Without this, everything else is impossible. | A tingling awareness. Feeling someone's intent before they move. Knowing a storm is coming before the clouds form. |
| **Draw** | Pull energy inward from the environment, filling your reserves. | Meditation, breathing exercises, or simply standing in a place of power and letting it fill you. Some places have more energy than others. |
| **Circulate** | Move energy through your body efficiently. Better circulation = more power, less waste, less strain. | The difference between a novice and a master isn't how much energy they have — it's how little they waste. |
| **Release** | Push energy outward or into an object. The basic offensive/utility action. | The first time you Release, it's clumsy — a burst of force with no direction. Training shapes it into something precise. |
| **Still** | Suppress your energy signature completely. Become invisible to those who Sense. | Dangerous — while Stilled, you have no passive defense. You're invisible but naked. The assassin's trade. |

Every warrior, mage, thief, and healer learns these same five things. The shared foundation means:
- Everyone understands the basics of everyone else's power
- A warrior can Sense a mage's Release coming and brace for it
- A thief's Still is detectable by someone with strong enough Sense
- Combat between practitioners is a conversation in a shared language

### The Five Attunements

Your natural attunement emerges from WHO YOU ARE — not chosen, discovered. A brave person's energy naturally flows inward. A curious person's energy naturally reaches outward. You can learn other attunements, but your natural one is always strongest.

#### Body (Internal Enhancement)

**What it does:** Draw energy INTO yourself. Harden skin, sharpen reflexes, strengthen muscles, accelerate healing. Your body becomes the weapon.

**Who has it:** Warriors, brawlers, laborers, athletes. People who are physical, direct, and present in their body.

**Personality link:** Brave, stubborn, physical, enduring. People who face things head-on.

**Progression:**
| Stage | Capability |
|---|---|
| Awakened | Slightly stronger/faster than normal. Can take a hit that would drop someone else. |
| Practiced | Visibly superhuman. Can bend iron, outrun a horse in a sprint, shrug off minor wounds. |
| Focused | Skin can turn a blade's edge. Reflexes faster than thought. Can fight for hours without tiring. |
| Tempered | Strikes carry enough force to crack stone. Can heal minor wounds in minutes. Senses enhanced (Body + Sense adjacency). |
| Masterful | Personal technique developed. A master swordsman's cut carries so much focused energy it can split a shield, an armor plate, a magical barrier. |
| Transcendent | The body transcends normal limits. A single punch reshapes terrain. Survived wounds that should have killed them three times over. Legend-tier. Requires a vow. |

**Why it matches magic:** A Transcendent Body practitioner doesn't cast spells — but their strike carries the same energy a mage would shape into a fireball, concentrated into a fist-sized point. The energy is identical. The application is different. A master swordsman's cut isn't "just" physical — it's energy-enhanced to a degree that makes it supernatural.

#### World (External Manipulation)

**What it does:** Push energy INTO external reality. Reshape matter, project force, conjure elements, manipulate the environment. The world becomes your instrument.

**Who has it:** Mages, scholars, artificers, alchemists. People who study, experiment, and think in systems.

**Personality link:** Curious, analytical, imaginative, ambitious. People who want to understand and reshape.

**Progression:**
| Stage | Capability |
|---|---|
| Awakened | Can nudge small objects. Light a candle. Feel the composition of materials. |
| Practiced | Elemental manipulation at small scale. Fire, water, earth, air — depending on environment and training. Can reinforce or weaken structures. |
| Focused | Full elemental control. Fireballs, stone walls, water redirection. Can sense the energy structure of objects and environments. |
| Tempered | Complex manipulations — reshaping matter, combining elements, affecting weather in a small area. Can enchant objects temporarily. |
| Masterful | Personal technique developed. A fire-specialist creates flames that burn specific things and leave others untouched. A stone-shaper builds structures that maintain themselves. |
| Transcendent | Reality bends. Terrain reshaping. Permanent enchantments. Weather control over a region. A single mage can match an army — but only within their specialty. Requires a vow. |

**Limitation:** World attunement requires UNDERSTANDING. You can't manipulate what you don't comprehend. A mage who's never studied fire can't control it. This makes study and knowledge prerequisites for power — a natural synergy with the scholar personality.

**Vulnerability:** World practitioners are physically unenhanced. Their bodies are mortal. A warrior who closes distance is terrifying. A mage needs space, preparation, and awareness.

#### Void (Suppression/Stealth)

**What it does:** Suppress energy — yours and the world's awareness of you. You don't become invisible to EYES — you become invisible to the world's ATTENTION. People's gazes slide past you. Footsteps don't register. Memory of your presence fades.

**Who has it:** Assassins, thieves, spies, scouts. People who survive by not being noticed.

**Personality link:** Deceptive, cautious, patient, independent. People who watch before acting.

**Progression:**
| Stage | Capability |
|---|---|
| Awakened | Hard to notice in a crowd. People forget you were there. |
| Practiced | Can move unseen in dim light. Footsteps make no sound when focused. Can suppress your energy signature completely (invisible to Sense). |
| Focused | True stealth — you can cross an open field and not be noticed. Memory suppression: people who saw you struggle to describe you later. |
| Tempered | Can extend suppression to objects (silence a door, suppress a weapon's presence). First-strike from stealth carries devastating force (all suppressed energy released at once). |
| Masterful | Personal technique developed. An assassin who can walk through a crowded court and kill a king without anyone noticing until the body falls. |
| Transcendent | Exist in the gaps of reality. You are genuinely not-there in a way that transcends stealth — you've become a blind spot in the world itself. Requires a vow. |

**The tradeoff:** While suppressed, you have NO passive defense. No enhanced body. No environmental manipulation. If detected, you're just a person with a knife. The Void practitioner must end things in one strike or escape immediately.

**The first-strike principle:** All that suppressed energy can be Released in a single devastating blow. A Void assassin's first attack from stealth is the most lethal single strike in the power system — but only the first. After that, they're exposed.

#### Sense (Perception/Information)

**What it does:** Listen to the energy in everything around you. Feel vibrations, read intentions, detect hidden things, predict movements. You don't act on the world — you UNDERSTAND it.

**Who has it:** Scouts, strategists, investigators, trackers. People who observe and analyze.

**Personality link:** Perceptive, empathetic, observant, intuitive. People who notice what others miss.

**Progression:**
| Stage | Capability |
|---|---|
| Awakened | Gut feelings that are always right. Can tell when someone is lying. Sense danger before it arrives. |
| Practiced | Read emotional states. Detect hidden people or objects. Track someone by their energy residue. |
| Focused | Feel the structure of nearby environments (hidden rooms, traps, weak points). Read combat intent — know what someone will do before they do it. |
| Tempered | Extended range. Can Sense across a building, a street, eventually a district. Detect power levels, attunement types, and emotional states at range. |
| Masterful | Personal technique developed. A strategist who can feel the flow of a battle and know exactly where to apply pressure. A tracker who reads the energy-echo of events that happened days ago. |
| Transcendent | Prophetic awareness. Glimpses of possible futures. The boundary between Sense and precognition blurs. Requires a vow — and the vow's cost is often sanity, because knowing everything is unbearable. |

**Combat role:** Sense practitioners rarely fight directly. They're force multipliers — they tell the warrior where to strike, warn the mage of incoming attacks, detect the assassin before they can act. In direct combat, their advantage is READING ENEMY INTENT — they see the attack before it comes. Combined with moderate Body or Void skills (adjacent attunements), this makes them elusive and hard to surprise.

**The card system expression:** Sense-based cards don't deal damage — they reveal information (see enemy deck, predict next move), buff allies (share awareness), and enable counters (preemptive blocks, perfect dodges).

#### Bond (Connection/Transfer)

**What it does:** Channel energy BETWEEN things. Transfer vitality, strengthen connections, reinforce objects, heal others. You are the bridge.

**Who has it:** Healers, smiths, teachers, diplomats. People who care about others more than themselves.

**Personality link:** Compassionate, loyal, protective, selfless. People who define themselves through relationships.

**Progression:**
| Stage | Capability |
|---|---|
| Awakened | Instinctive healing touch. Wounds close slightly faster under your hands. Objects you repair last longer. |
| Practiced | Intentional healing — mend broken bones, stop bleeding, cure minor illness. Reinforce a weapon or wall to be unnaturally durable. |
| Focused | Transfer energy between willing targets. Give your strength to a wounded ally. Take someone's pain into yourself (you feel it, they don't). |
| Tempered | Forge lasting connections between objects and energy (enchantment). Create wards that persist. Heal conditions, not just wounds — disease, poison, madness. |
| Masterful | Personal technique developed. A healer who can reconstruct a shattered limb (not regrow — reconstruct from the pieces). A smith who forges weapons with their own energy woven in, creating items of genuine power. |
| Transcendent | Reconnect the dying to life. Forge connections between souls. Create artifacts that persist for centuries. Requires a vow — and the vow almost always involves sacrifice. Bond transcendents give something up permanently for others. |

**The cost:** Bond practitioners absorb what they heal. Every wound mended is partially felt. Every pain transferred leaves a residue. High-level Bond users accumulate the suffering of everyone they've saved. This is the attunement's built-in tragedy — the healer is always in pain.

**Combat role:** Support. Bond practitioners keep others alive, reinforce equipment mid-battle, create temporary energy-shields, and in desperate moments, transfer their own vitality to save a dying ally. They can also disrupt enemy connections — sever the energy flowing between a Manipulator and their puppet, break the enchantment on a weapon.

### The Adjacency System

```
        Body
       /    \
    Bond      World
      |        |
    Sense ─── Void
```

Your natural attunement = 100%. Adjacent = 75%. Two steps = 50%. Opposite = 30%.

| Your Type | Adjacent (75%) | Two Steps (50%) | Opposite (30%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Body | Bond, World | Sense, Void | — |
| World | Body, Void | Bond, Sense | — |
| Void | World, Sense | Body, Bond | — |
| Sense | Void, Bond | World, Body | — |
| Bond | Sense, Body | Void, World | — |

**Design note:** Unlike Nen's hexagon with a clear opposite, this pentagon has no single "opposite." Every type has two weakest connections at 50%. This means nobody is truly terrible at anything — just mediocre at their weakest areas. The gap between 100% and 50% is significant but not crippling.

### The Vow System

**Voluntary restrictions multiply power.** Inspired directly by Nen's conditions.

**How it works:**
1. A practitioner at Focused stage or higher can take a vow
2. The vow is a permanent, binding restriction on how they use their power
3. The more severe the restriction, the greater the power multiplier
4. Breaking the vow has catastrophic consequences (power shatters, backlash injury, death)

**Examples:**

| Vow | Restriction | Power Granted | Breaking Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protector's Oath | Weapon only drawn against threats to the innocent. Cannot be used for personal gain or aggression. | +3 damage vs threats, +2 block when defending others | The weapon shatters. All Body attunement drops to Awakened permanently. |
| Truthbound | Cannot speak a lie, ever. Not even a white lie. Not even omission through silence. | Supernaturally persuasive. Words carry energy — spoken commands have weight. | Voice is lost permanently. |
| One Target | Can only use full power against a single named enemy. All attacks against others are at base level. | Quadruple damage against the named target. | If the target dies by other means, the practitioner loses all power above Practiced. |
| The Hermit's Seal | Cannot use power within any settlement. Only in wilderness. | Double all effects in wild/untamed areas. | If power is used in a settlement, immediate unconsciousness + permanent attunement reduction. |

**Narrative weight:** Taking a vow should be a SCENE, not a menu choice. The character speaks it aloud. The world hears. Something shifts — the energy responds. The restriction is real from that moment. There's no undo.

## System 2: Worship Power (Devotion)

### The Source

Completely separate from mortal power. Doesn't use the world's energy. Comes from **directed human consciousness** — belief, fear, love, awe, reverence, obsession, habit. Anything that occupies a human mind toward a specific target generates Devotion.

### Core Rules

**1. Directed belief generates power.**
Any sustained mental/emotional focus on a specific being, object, or concept generates Devotion. Prayer is the most efficient form, but it's not the only one:
- A mother thinking of her dead child generates Devotion toward the child's memory
- A nation fearing their king generates Devotion toward the king
- A scholar obsessing over an artifact generates Devotion toward the artifact
- Soldiers chanting a war-hero's name before battle generate Devotion toward the hero

**2. The character of belief shapes the power.**

| What People Feel | Power Manifestation | Aesthetic |
|---|---|---|
| Love / gratitude | Protection, healing, warmth, sanctuary, calming presence | Golden, warm |
| Fear / dread | Paralysis, shadow, compulsion, command, nightmares | Dark, cold |
| Awe / wonder | Inspiration, enhancement of others, radiance, beauty | Bright, overwhelming |
| Devotion / faith | Versatile — amplifies whatever the believers imagine you to be | Reflects the believer's image |
| Fanaticism / obsession | Raw force, unstable, corrupting to both giver and receiver | Burning, consuming |
| Habit / tradition | Steady, reliable, weak, slow to change | Muted, ambient |
| Resentment / grudging respect | Uncomfortable power that works against your nature | Twisted, ironic |

**3. Quality > quantity.** One true believer with genuine faith generates more Devotion than a thousand hollow worshippers going through motions. (The Pratchett principle: Om with one genuine believer was stronger than the entire Omnian church's hollow prayers.)

**4. Hollow worship feeds the institution.** If people worship the CHURCH but not the god, the church gains power. If people fear the CROWN but not the king, the crown gains power. Worship can be accidentally redirected. Institutions, objects, and concepts can become unintentional recipients.

**5. Most people don't understand this.** Common people see supernatural leadership as divine right, bloodline magic, or "just how things are." Only rare scholars theorize about the mechanism. The player-god knows because they've experienced it firsthand. This knowledge is a strategic asset.

### The Spectrum of Divinity

See [god-hierarchy.md](god-hierarchy.md) for the full 5-tier system (Reputation → Historical Figure → Power → Deity → Concept God).

**Note on origin vs tier:** The three god origin types described below (concept, worship, ascended) describe HOW a god was born. The 5 tiers describe how powerful they are. These are orthogonal — a god of any origin can exist at various tiers. A worship-born god could grow to rival a concept god in influence. An ascended mortal could fall back to Tier 1 if their worshippers forget them. Origin is history. Tier is current power.

### How Kings and Leaders Work

A newly crowned king inherits residual Devotion from the institution of the throne. They start with a "slight edge." As they rule, the nature of their rule shapes what grows:

**A just king** (loved, trusted):
- Over years → protection powers develop
- Crops grow better in their kingdom. Walls hold longer. Soldiers fight harder.
- Nobody knows WHY — it just seems like a blessed reign
- If they're overthrown, the protection fades and the kingdom visibly suffers

**A tyrant** (feared, obeyed):
- Over years → compulsion/dread powers develop
- People obey before he speaks. Shadows seem darker in his throne room.
- He can sense betrayal forming — the fear tells him
- But fear-Devotion is brittle. A single crack of defiance can cascade into revolution, and the power collapses suddenly rather than gradually

**A conqueror** (awed, mythologized):
- Over years → battle-enhancement powers
- Presence on the battlefield is worth a thousand soldiers
- But the power depends on WINNING. A single major defeat can shatter the awe.

### How Gods Work

**Concept gods:**
- Born because the concept exists (fire, death, the sea, time)
- Don't need worship to exist
- Without worship: impersonal force. Fire burns. Death takes. No awareness, no personality.
- With worship: the concept gains personality shaped by how people think of it. A death god worshipped with grief is melancholy. A death god worshipped with terror is monstrous. Same concept, different worship, different god.
- Cannot be killed unless the concept itself ceases to exist

**Worship gods:**
- Created by accumulated belief in ANYTHING — a sacred tree, a legendary sword, a river, an idea
- Gain awareness gradually as Devotion accumulates
- Entirely dependent on worship. No believers = fading = death
- Their nature is defined by what people believe about them. Change the stories, change the god.
- Can be intentionally created by someone who understands the mechanism

**Ascended mortals:**
- Mortal who accumulated enough worship to persist beyond death
- Retain mortal personality (including flaws, loves, grudges)
- Have BOTH residual mortal power AND worship power
- Most relatable gods — and most dangerous, because they understand mortal ambition

### The Player-God

- Was once worshipped. Doesn't remember by whom or as what.
- Currently near-zero Devotion. Rebuilding through the deeds of vessels.
- Can spend accumulated Influence (earned from past runs) to intervene subtly
- Domain unknown — crystallizes from accumulated play patterns
- Discovering what you WERE is a late-game revelation
- What you're BECOMING may be different from what you were

### How Both Systems Interact

The two power systems are orthogonal — a being can have one, both, or neither:

| Mortal Power | Worship Power | What You Are |
|---|---|---|
| None | None | Ordinary person |
| High | None | Master warrior/mage/etc — dangerous but not political |
| None | High | Beloved king / feared tyrant — powerful but physically mortal |
| High | High | Legendary warrior-king, cult leader mage — terrifying |
| None | Transcendent | Full god with no combat training — acts through followers/miracles |
| Transcendent | High | Ascended mortal — the most dangerous beings in the world |

**Key interactions:**
- A Body-attuned warrior who becomes a feared king gets dread aura ON TOP of superhuman strength
- A World-attuned mage who becomes a concept god gets domain-locked divine power AND mortal magic
- A Void-attuned assassin worshipped as a legend becomes supernaturally invisible — but the legend itself makes them a target (the worship power and the stealth power work against each other)
- The player-god's vessels get mortal power through training AND access to the god's worship power through divine intervention

### How Both Systems Map to Cards

See [combat.md](combat.md) for full card system details.

**Quick summary:**

| Layer | Source | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| **Mortal deck** | Attunement + equipment + skills + traits + wounds | Reliable. Always available. Grows through training. |
| **Worship deck** | Belief directed at the character | Volatile. Appears when worship grows. Disappears if belief fades. Powerful but unreliable. |
| **Divine cards** | Player-god's Influence | Rare. Expensive. The god's direct intervention through the vessel. |
