# VESTIGE — Thread Map

*Threads are the connective tissue that makes world states feel alive. A world state describes a condition. A thread describes a story moving through that condition.*

---

## Main Threads (span the full tree, connect to endings)

### 1. The Worship Economy
How mortals and gods relate. From first worship to divine ecology collapse to new covenant. Every state touches this. The trophic cascade model applies: gods are keystone species in the worship ecosystem. Remove one and the downstream effects cascade.

**Key question:** What should the relationship between mortals and gods look like?
**Runs through:** Every branch. Manifests differently in each.
**Endpoints:** Genuine peace (negotiated divine relationship), Godless Age (gods removed), Covenant World (formal treaty), Second Genesis (old gods die, new ones form), Silence (divine ecology collapses entirely).

### 2. The Faction Power Balance
Who governs, how, at what cost. The five Era 2 factions (Forge, Senate, Rootwalker network, Consortium, and whichever new factions emerge) compete, merge, dissolve, and reform. The balance at any moment determines what kind of world this is.

**Key question:** Can power be shared, or does someone always end up on top?
**Runs through:** Every non-divine branch. The economic thread (Exchange→Syndicate→Corporate) and military thread (Iron→Crusade→Empire) are sub-expressions of this.
**Endpoints:** Dominance (one faction wins), Alliance with cost (two factions, one excluded), Fragmentation (nobody wins), Genuine Peace (everyone compromises).

### 3. The Player-God's Identity
What kind of god are you becoming? Domain crystallizes from accumulated choices. This is the meta-thread — the player discovers it by watching what they keep choosing.

**Key question:** After centuries of watching this world, what kind of god did you become?
**Runs through:** Everything. It IS the game.
**Endpoints:** Whatever domain the player crystallized. Mercy, dominion, knowledge, craft, connection, silence.

## Major Sub-Threads (can resolve independently, touch main threads when they intersect)

### 4. The Tideborn (Era 2 late → Era 3+)
Foreign civilization. Two waves. The Unmaking. The Listener.
- **Internal resolution:** The Listener sacrifices itself / survives / teaches / dissolves. Taiu learns the truth / doesn't. The inner circle succeeds / fails.
- **Main thread intersection:** Divine ecology (the Unmaking threatens all gods), faction balance (Tideborn destabilize existing power), player identity (the Listener mirrors the player-god).
- **Sub-sub-threads:** The Listener's fate, Taiu's reckoning, Rua's burden, the inner circle's hunt.

### 5. The Ashfield God (Era 1 → resolves by Era 3-4)
Born from collective fear. Everyone's fault. Not malicious — confused.
- **Internal resolution:** Destroyed, reshaped through unworship, befriended, ignored until it's too large, or consumed by the Tideborn's Unmaking.
- **Main thread intersection:** Worship economy (can you change a god by changing the story?), faction balance (who controls the Ashfield controls fear), player identity (how you treat a confused newborn god says everything about what kind of god you are).

### 6. The Forge Identity Crisis (Era 1 → resolves by Era 3-4)
What is craft when the walls come down? Thornwall's identity is built on isolation and excellence. Every Iron branch tests whether that identity can survive contact with the outside.
- **Internal resolution:** Diaspora (scatter the knowledge), Reformation (open the forges), Crusade (turn defense into offense), Slow death (the walls hold but the people leave).
- **Main thread intersection:** Faction balance (the Forge is the military power), player identity (what you do with Thornwall determines whether you're a god of tradition or change).
- **Sub-sub-threads:** The True Forge's exile, the young smiths' rebellion, the Wall Keepers' loyalty.

### 7. The Song's Fate (Era 1 → resolves in Wandering branches)
The Song of the Cracked Stone — Vaya's composition — either unites or fragments the Rootwalker network.
- **Internal resolution:** The Song holds (Great Meeting → Republic), the Song fragments (Song Breaks → Scattering), the Song evolves (Walking Meeting → merged with Tideborn songs).
- **Main thread intersection:** Faction balance (the Rootwalker network is the information infrastructure), worship economy (the Drift feeds on movement; if movement stops, the Drift fades).

## Sub-Threads Within States (resolve within one era, give states texture)

### Exchange Branch Sub-Threads:
- **The Ledger's Discovery** (Syndicate Age) — Senna opens the Ledger, discovers the founding myth is imperfect. Can resolve as: she publishes it (People's Revolt), she keeps the secret (Corporate Dominion), she reforms from within (unlikely, the Consortium resists).
- **The Scale's Captivity** (Syndicate Age → Corporate Dominion) — the commerce god being gamed by its own worshippers. Can resolve as: the Scale breaks free (disrupts all contracts), the Scale accepts domestication (becomes a tool), the Scale dies (commerce loses its divine foundation).
- **Col's Debt** (Syndicate Age) — a debt-born boy who can't imagine freedom. His thread is small but it's the human face of "debt is citizenship."

### Iron Branch Sub-Threads:
- **The True Forge** (Age of Iron → wherever they scatter) — purist smiths in exile. They have the old techniques. They won't share them. The thread resolves when someone decides whether purity or survival matters more.
- **The Foot** (Age of Iron) — the unofficial town at the base of the mountain. A culture of defiance. The thread resolves when the walls crack and The Foot is either absorbed or vindicated.

### Questions Branch Sub-Threads:
- **The Unworship Movement** (Age of Questions → wherever belief becomes weaponized) — can you change a god by changing the story? The thread resolves when someone successfully (or catastrophically) reshapes a god through redirected belief.

### Wandering Branch Sub-Threads:
- **The Crossroads Culture** (Age of Wandering → wherever walkers settle) — the places where walkers meet become permanent settlements. The thread resolves when the crossroads decide if they're towns or just meeting points.

## Design Principle

Every world state should:
1. **Touch at least one main thread** — the state is a moment in an ongoing story, not an isolated condition
2. **Contain at least one sub-thread that can resolve on its own** — stories happening whether the player watches or not
3. **Have sub-threads that INTERSECT** — when the Ledger thread crosses the Scale thread crosses Col's thread, the state feels alive

The boring states are boring because they describe conditions without threads. "Nobody is innovating" is a condition. "Maret watches her grandson live a life her sons died to make possible and can't decide if it was worth it" is a thread.

## The Boundary Principle (from brine pool research)

**Boundaries between extremes are ecosystems, not problems.**

Deep-ocean brine pools are lakes of toxic brine at the ocean floor — anything that enters dies. But at the BOUNDARY where the poison meets the water, the richest ecosystem in the deep sea gathers. Mussels, tube worms, crabs, sharks. Not in the pool. Not away from it. At the exact edge.

The Ashfield is a brine pool. The fear-god's presence is toxic. Nobody lives inside. But the Ash Tenders live at the boundary — adapted to the gradient, thriving because of it, not despite it.

**Design implications:**
1. Every state should have at least one boundary — the line between two forces that can't mix
2. The most interesting characters live AT the boundary — Bridge Folk, Ash Tenders, The Foot, Lael with her bark hand, Dev the atheist who talks to gods
3. "Solving" a boundary (removing the toxicity, collapsing the gradient) might destroy the boundary ecosystem — the cure kills the mussels. The unworship movement removing the Ashfield god might destroy the Ash Tender culture that exists because of the boundary.
4. The question "what lives at the boundary?" is always more interesting than "which side wins?"

The boundary is the ecosystem. The edge is where the story lives.
