# VESTIGE — The God System

*You are the vestige of something. Each life shows you more of the shape.*

---

## The Player-God

### Starting State

You wake with nothing. No name. No domain. No worshippers. A shape where memory should be.

The player-god was once Tier 4 or 5 (see [god-hierarchy.md](god-hierarchy.md)) before falling to Tier 0 — completely forgotten. Now they wake with nothing.

You know:
- You were something once
- You can feel mortal lives below you
- Some of them are... open. You could step through.
- Everything else is gone

### What the God IS (to the player)

The god is the player's persistent identity across runs. Mechanically:
- **You select vessels** (newborns to embody)
- **You control characters** (make all choices, fight all combats)
- **You persist between deaths** (memories carry forward)
- **You accumulate power** (worship, influence, knowledge)
- **You discover your nature** (domain crystallization)

The god is NOT a separate gameplay layer during lives. When you're in a vessel, you ARE them. The god perspective exists in the between — vessel selection, world overview, divine intervention moments.

## The God Interface

### Between Lives

The god interface is visually and tonally distinct from the mortal experience. The mortal layer is intimate, text-heavy, warm with detail. The god layer is vast, sparse, panoramic. You're looking down at a world, not living in it.

**What you see:**
- The world overview (regions, factions, threats — as much as your power allows)
- The summary of your last life's impact
- The passage of time and what changed
- Available vessels (candidates to embody)
- Your accumulated resources (worship, influence, knowledge)

**What you can do:**
- Review past lives and their legacy
- Study the world state (costs influence at higher detail)
- Select your next vessel
- Choose where to focus your attention for the coming life

### Vessel Selection

**Run 1 (weak god):** 3 candidates. Bare impressions only.
> *A child. Somewhere cold. Feels strong.*

**Run 3 (growing god):** 4-5 candidates. Inherent traits visible.
> *A girl born in Thornwall. Inherent: naturally quick, keen eyesight. Family: blacksmith father, trader mother. The forge smoke is in her hair already.*

**Run 7 (established god):** 5-6 candidates. Full traits, family, cultural context visible.
> *A boy born in the Exile camps beyond the Northern border. Inherent: prophetic dreams (RARE), frail constitution. Family: grandmother only — the rest died in the schism you caused three lives ago. Culture: Exile (rootless, adaptive, bitter). The dreams have already started. He cries in his sleep.*

**Run 12+ (powerful god):** Full information. Can see attunement potential, relationship possibilities, proximity to past-life legacy. Some candidates are DESCENDANTS of past characters.

### Divine Intervention (During Lives)

Rare. Costly. The god can occasionally reach through to the vessel.

**Types of intervention:**
| Intervention | Influence Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Whisper | Low | A vague feeling — "don't go that way." Adds a choice the character wouldn't otherwise have. |
| Vision | Medium | A flash of knowledge from a past life — a map, a name, a warning. Adds specific information. |
| Surge | High | A burst of divine energy — one combat turn with enhanced stats. The character feels something foreign in their blood. |
| Revelation | Very High | The character realizes they're being guided. Gains the "Touched" trait. NPCs notice. Everything changes. |

**The cost of overuse:** Too many interventions and the vessel becomes visibly marked. The "Touched" trait is double-edged — it grants divine sensitivity but makes the character a target. Other gods notice. The character might resent being a puppet.

**The ideal use:** 0-2 interventions per life. Saved for critical moments. The god trusts the mortal to live their own life most of the time.

## Divine Resources

### Worship

The god's core power source. Grows when characters:
- Pray to the god directly (requires someone knowing the god exists)
- Attribute their success to divine guidance
- Spread the god's name/story
- Build shrines, start cults, inspire faith

Worship decreases when:
- Followers die without replacing themselves
- A character the god guided fails publicly
- Another god actively suppresses your worship
- Time passes and memory fades

**Worship determines:** How much the god can see during vessel selection. How many interventions are affordable. How strongly the god's domain manifests.

### Influence

Spendable resource for divine actions. Regenerates slowly based on worship level.

**Spent on:**
- Vessel selection (seeing more detail about candidates)
- World overview (examining factions, threats, distant regions)
- Divine intervention during lives
- Blessings (granting divine trait to vessel)

### Knowledge

Permanent. Never lost. The god's accumulated understanding of the world.

**Unlocked through:**
- Living lives in different regions (you know the geography)
- Encountering factions (you know their goals and methods)
- Fighting enemies (you know their weaknesses)
- Discovering lore (you know the history)
- Experiencing the power system (you know how it works)

**Knowledge manifests as:** The PLAYER knowing things. No mechanical storage needed — the player's brain is the god's memory. The Fear and Hunger principle.

But ALSO: some knowledge unlocks new options. Knowing a monster's weakness might add a choice during combat ("Aim for the third eye — you've seen this creature die before"). Knowing a faction's secret might add a dialogue option. These are authored events gated by knowledge-flags.

### Legacy

The accumulated reputation of all past characters. Not a number — a narrative footprint.

**Legacy manifests as:**
- NPCs telling stories about past characters (accurate or distorted)
- Monuments, buildings, artifacts from past lives
- Faction attitudes shaped by past involvement
- Descendants who carry echoes of past characters' traits
- The god's own name/title forming from accumulated deeds

## Domain Crystallization

### How It Works

The game tracks the player's accumulated choices across all runs along several axes:

| Axis | Positive Pole | Negative Pole |
|---|---|---|
| Mercy vs Justice | Forgave, healed, protected | Punished, enforced, judged |
| Knowledge vs Action | Studied, learned, planned | Fought, built, conquered |
| Connection vs Independence | Built communities, formed bonds | Acted alone, self-reliant |
| Creation vs Destruction | Made things, grew things, healed | Broke things, cleared paths, purged |
| Order vs Freedom | Established systems, maintained stability | Disrupted hierarchies, liberated |

The god's domain emerges from whichever axes the player consistently leans toward.

### Example Domains

| Accumulated Pattern | Domain | Title (earned by the world) |
|---|---|---|
| Mercy + Connection + Creation | God of Hearth/Shelter | "The Warm Threshold" |
| Justice + Action + Order | God of Law/Judgment | "The Iron Scale" |
| Knowledge + Independence | God of Wisdom/Foresight | "The Unblinking Eye" |
| Action + Destruction | God of War/Conquest | "The Red March" |
| Mercy + Knowledge + Connection | God of Healing/Memory | "The One Who Remembers" |
| Freedom + Creation | God of Art/Inspiration | "The Unnamed Muse" |
| Destruction + Independence + Action | God of Endings/Entropy | "The Last Word" |

### The Emergence (not selection)

The domain is NEVER chosen in a menu. It emerges:
- Around run 8-10: NPCs start describing you in consistent terms. "There's something about you... a warmth. Like you carry a hearth inside."
- Around run 12-15: Your worship power begins manifesting in domain-consistent ways. If you're becoming a god of mercy, your worship cards trend toward protection/healing.
- Around run 15-20: The god interface itself changes aesthetically. A god of hearth sees warm golden tones. A god of war sees crimson.
- **The naming moment:** When the domain is crystallized, the world gives you a name. Not chosen. Earned. This is one of the game's peak emotional moments.

### What You Were vs What You're Becoming

Scattered across the world: fragments of who you used to be.
- An old shrine with a symbol you don't recognize (but the god in you shivers)
- A forgotten prayer written in a dead language
- A scholar who's researched nameless gods and has theories
- A concept god who remembers you ("You were... different then")

**Discovering your past is a late-game thread.** What you WERE might conflict with what you're BECOMING:
- You were a god of war who keeps choosing mercy
- You were a god of knowledge who keeps choosing connection
- You were a god of destruction who keeps choosing to build

The tension between past nature and present choices is a story that writes itself.

## Other Gods

### The Pantheon

The world has existing gods. They have domains, personalities, worshippers, and agendas. The player-god is entering an ecosystem, not an empty field.

**God interactions:**
- **Ally:** Shared interests. They help your vessels, you spread their worship.
- **Rival:** Competing domains. A new god of war threatens the existing one.
- **Patron:** An established god takes interest in you. Offers power in exchange for service.
- **Hunter:** A god that sees you as a threat and actively works against your vessels.
- **Indifferent:** Most gods, especially concept gods, don't care about you. Yet.

### How Gods Notice You

- **Run 1-5:** You're too small to notice. A whisper among whispers.
- **Run 6-10:** Concept gods in your area of activity sense something. "There's a new thread in the weave."
- **Run 11-15:** Worship gods feel the competition. If your domain overlaps with theirs, they react.
- **Run 16+:** You're a known entity. Gods approach you with offers, threats, or curiosity.

### God vs God Conflict

Gods rarely fight directly (mutually assured diminishment — worship competition weakens both sides). They fight through:
- **Competing worshippers** (your cult vs their church)
- **Champion vessels** (your guided mortal vs their chosen hero)
- **Domain interference** (a god of silence making your war-drums fall quiet)
- **Worship theft** (redirecting your followers' belief to themselves)

The divine political layer emerges naturally in the late game.

## The Endgame Question

After 20+ runs, hundreds of in-world years, dozens of lives guided:

**"You've been watching this world for centuries. What kind of god are you?"**

The answer is written in:
- The world's history (shaped by your choices)
- Your domain (crystallized from your patterns)
- Your name (given by the world based on what you became)
- The state of the pantheon (who you allied with, rivaled, ignored)
- The people who worship you (and what their belief has made you)

There is no "winning." There is no final boss. There is only the accumulation of lives lived and the world they created. The game ends when you decide to stop playing — and the world you built is the evidence of everything you were.

Or maybe it doesn't end. Maybe there's always one more life. One more vessel. One more chance to see what the world becomes next.

*The ??? is the point.* That ambiguity is intentional — the unanswerable question at the heart of divinity. What IS the point of an eternal being who watches mortals live and die? The game doesn't answer. The player's accumulated choices are the closest thing to an answer they'll get.
